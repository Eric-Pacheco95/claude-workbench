## Banking & Regulatory

**AML** — Anti-Money Laundering; regulations and processes to detect and prevent money laundering activity

**KYC** — Know Your Customer; the process of verifying client identity and assessing suitability before onboarding

**KYB** — Know Your Business; KYC equivalent for corporate/business clients

**OSFI** — Office of the Superintendent of Financial Institutions; Canada's federal banking regulator

**PIPEDA** — Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act; Canada's federal privacy law for private sector

**PCI-DSS** — Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard; security standard for organizations handling credit card data

**SOX** — Sarbanes-Oxley Act; US law governing financial reporting and internal controls (Canadian banks with US listings comply)

**FINTRAC** — Financial Transactions and Reports Analysis Centre; Canada's financial intelligence unit (AML/CTF reporting)

**CTF** — Counter-Terrorism Financing; regulatory obligation to detect and report suspected terrorism financing

**RTR** — Real-Time Rail; Canada's upcoming real-time payment infrastructure

**EFT** — Electronic Funds Transfer; generic term for electronic money movement

**SWIFT** — Society for Worldwide Interbank Financial Telecommunication; messaging network for international transfers

**BNS / BMO / CIBC / RBC / TD** — The Big Five Canadian banks (Scotiabank, Bank of Montreal, Canadian Imperial Bank of Commerce, Royal Bank of Canada, Toronto-Dominion Bank)

## Process & Methodology

**BA** — Business Analyst; translates business needs into requirements and user stories

**BSA** — Business Systems Analyst; BA with stronger technical/systems focus

**DoR** — Definition of Ready; criteria a story must meet before sprint planning

**DoD** — Definition of Done; criteria a story must meet before it is considered complete

**AC** — Acceptance Criteria; conditions that must be satisfied for a user story to be accepted

**UAT** — User Acceptance Testing; business-side testing to confirm requirements are met

**SME** — Subject Matter Expert; domain expert consulted during requirements gathering

**RAID** — Risks, Assumptions, Issues, Dependencies; common project tracking framework

**ADR** — Architecture Decision Record; document capturing a significant architectural decision with context and rationale

**MoSCoW** — Must have / Should have / Could have / Won't have; requirements prioritization framework

**NFR** — Non-Functional Requirement; system quality attributes (performance, security, scalability)

## Technical

**API** — Application Programming Interface; contract for how systems communicate

**REST** — Representational State Transfer; architectural style for APIs using HTTP

**ETL** — Extract, Transform, Load; data pipeline pattern

**IAM** — Identity and Access Management; systems controlling user authentication and authorization

**SSO** — Single Sign-On; authentication allowing one login to access multiple systems

**MFA** — Multi-Factor Authentication; requiring 2+ verification factors for login

**PII** — Personally Identifiable Information; data that can identify an individual

**SDLC** — Software Development Life Cycle; the process of planning, creating, testing, and deploying software
